LEANDER — Coach Chris’s phone has reportedly not stopped ringing since word spread that the Rogers and Cargill families were joining Venom.

What began as a normal onboarding process quickly became a full-scale communications event involving roster questions, old softball stories, new family expectations, uniform details, scheduling, and several calls that apparently started with, “So I heard…”

“The phone is no longer a device. It is now an assistant coach,” one completely fictional front-office source said.

Most Calls Lead Back to Rogers and Cargill

According to the parody call log, a majority of the week’s activity involved onboarding the Rogers and Cargill families. The rest involved people calling to ask why so many people were calling about the Rogers and Cargill families.

The situation was further complicated by the Cargills’ running “Brian Tax” joke—the family’s name for the extra explanation, curiosity and occasional side-eye they feel can follow any mention of Brian Rogers or their early select-softball days with the Bananas.

Whether that reaction is real, exaggerated, outdated or simply a byproduct of competitive youth softball remains outside the investigative jurisdiction of this fake newsroom.

Venom’s First Opponent: Bombers Meza

As if the opening matchup needed more plot, Venom’s first opponent is Bombers Meza.

The family’s version of events is that Kassidy Cargill had drawn serious interest from people around the Bombers organization and was viewed by some as a strong possible fit for their future. She then did not attend the scheduled tryout—an episode the parody desk is dramatically labeling “The Ghosting of Meza.”

There was no cinematic betrayal, no secret flight out of town and no transfer-portal limousine. There was simply a no-show, a different team decision, and enough softball gossip to power three tournament weekends.

“She did not ghost an entire organization. She missed a tryout. But that headline gets fewer clicks,” the Venom satire editor admitted.

From Possible Bomber to Opening Opponent

The result is a made-for-softball opening storyline: a player once discussed as a possible Bombers fit now lining up across the field in Venom colors.

Any suggestion that Kassidy was guaranteed a Bombers spot, or that the entire organization expected her arrival, would be impossible for this parody page to verify. The safer—and funnier—version is that enough people believed the match made sense that her no-show became part of the family lore.

Kassidy herself is reportedly far less interested in the narrative than the adults. Her official position remains: throw the ball, hit the ball, catch the ball, and let everyone else build a documentary around the parking lot.

Coach Chris Returns to the Griddle

Coach Chris was last seen wearing an apron, cooking breakfast tacos, onboarding two families, answering a fifth consecutive call about the same rumor, and reviewing a Bombers scouting report that simply read: “Expect awkward handshakes.”

He then attempted to silence his phone. The phone declined.

As of publication, the Rogers and Cargill onboarding process was nearly complete, the Brian Tax remained fictional, Bombers Meza remained Venom’s first opponent, and Kassidy had still not issued a formal apology for failing to appear in a tryout storyline she never agreed to star in.
