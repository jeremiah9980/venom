Why a board

The Board's Purpose

The Board of Directors exists to give the organization formal governance structure. It votes on major decisions, holds the Co-Directors accountable, and provides the credibility that helps attract coaches, sponsors, and committed families.

The Board does not coach. Player selection, lineups, development decisions, and all on-field matters belong exclusively to the Head Coach. The Board's authority ends at the dugout gate.

"The Board exists to let our coaches do one thing: coach. Every obstacle that isn't softball — we handle it." — Cargill, Co-Director

How decisions get made

Voting at a Glance

Three voting categories govern how major decisions are made. Details are in the full Bylaws.

I
Ordinary Business
Simple majority · 3-member quorum · Tournaments, policy tweaks, staff appointments
II
Significant Decisions
Two-thirds majority · 4-member quorum · Budget, dues, seating/removing members
III
Foundational Matters
Unanimous vote · All members · Dissolution, Co-Director removal, core bylaw changes
